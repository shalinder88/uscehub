About this program

UAMS College of Medicine runs a Visiting Students Program at its Little Rock campus, open to M4 students at U.S. accredited medical schools. The program runs through the AAMC's national VSLO platform. Applications go through your home Dean's office, not directly to UAMS.

This is a clinical elective rotation, typically 4 weeks, with a maximum of 8 weeks. Required documents: completed core clerkships, a Dean's letter, and active health and malpractice coverage before arrival.

What's included

  • 4–8 weeks of structured clinical elective (typical: 4 weeks)
  • Placement in your chosen specialty department at UAMS Little Rock
  • Direct supervision by UAMS attending faculty
  • Rounds, case discussions, and bedside teaching participation
  • UAMS library, EMR, and educational resources access
  • Letter of recommendation eligibility (attending discretion)
  • Certificate of completion upon finishing the rotation

Who can apply

  • M4 students at U.S. accredited medical schools
  • Home institution participating in AAMC VSLO
  • Dean's written permission from your home institution

Who's not eligible

  • International medical graduates (IMGs) and international medical students
  • Longitudinal-integrated curriculum (LIC) students
  • Pre-clerkship students. Core clerkships must be complete first.

Required clerkships

Internal Medicine Surgery OB-GYN Pediatrics Psychiatry

Documents you'll need

  • Medical school transcript
  • Dean's letter of permission
  • USMLE Step 1 score report
  • Immunization records
  • Active malpractice coverage
  • Photo ID
  • Background check (via VSLO)
  • Drug screen documentation

How to apply

  1. Confirm eligibility with your home Dean's office.Verify your school participates in VSLO and that you have completed the five required core clerkships.
  2. Open the UAMS Visiting Students page.Review the current schedule, available specialties, and any UAMS-specific forms.
  3. Submit your application through the AAMC VSLO platform.Your home Dean's office handles the submission. Include the Dean's letter of permission.
  4. Wait for UAMS to assign your rotation.UAMS reviews applications and assigns placement in your requested specialty. Lead time varies by demand.
  5. Complete pre-arrival onboarding.Health clearance, malpractice attestation, and orientation are coordinated through VSLO and the UAMS office.

Available specialties

Rotations available across the specialties below. Availability varies by cycle.

Internal Medicine Surgery Pediatrics OB-GYN Psychiatry Emergency Medicine Radiology Anesthesia Neurology Pathology

Things to know before applying

  • M4 students only. M3 and earlier applications are not processed.
  • Application is via VSLO only. Direct submissions to UAMS will be redirected.
  • Maximum 8 weeks total. Cannot combine multiple rotations to exceed 8 weeks.
  • Specialty availability changes by cycle. Check current openings on the source page.

Frequently asked questions
Common questions
+
Can I apply if I'm an IMG graduate?

No. UAMS Visiting Students Program accepts only senior medical students currently enrolled at U.S. accredited medical schools.

Do I need a visa?

No. This program is for U.S. domestic medical students.

Can I do more than 8 weeks at UAMS?

No, the maximum is 8 weeks total across all visiting student rotations at UAMS.

Does UAMS provide housing?

UAMS does not provide on-campus housing. Most rotators arrange furnished short-term housing nearby.

Will I get a letter of recommendation?

LOR is at the discretion of your attending. UAMS does not guarantee an LOR.
