The Robert Larner, M.D. College of Medicine

Innovation in Medical Education, Research, and Clinical Care

Founded in 1822, the Robert Larner, M.D. College of Medicine at the University of Vermont is the seventh oldest medical school in the nation.

We at the Larner College of Medicine are dedicated to developing exceptional physicians and scientists by offering innovative curriculum designs, state-of-the-art research facilities, and clinical partnerships with leading health care institutions. Our College’s commitment to excellence has earned national recognition, attracting talented students, trainees, physicians, and researchers from across the country. With a focus on intercultural excellence, the Larner College of Medicine prides itself on cultivating an environment that uplifts and supports its faculty and student populations while advancing medical education, research, and patient care in Vermont and beyond. 

Welcome from Dean Richard L. Page, M.D.

Welcome to the UVM Robert Larner, M.D. College of Medicine. As the seventh oldest medical school in the nation, we take pride in our commitment to innovation in education. We invite you to embark on a transformative journey with us. - Richard L. Page, M.D. | Dean | UVM Chief Medical Affairs Officer

Research

The UVM Larner College of Medicine receives $90 - 100 million annually in research funding. Researchers in laboratory, clinical, and community settings work toward a greater understanding of disease and wellness, as well as new, more effective treatments.

Education

Our mission is to educate a diverse group of dedicated physicians and biomedical scientists. With an active-learning-based curriculum, we build students' practical skills, as well as their ability to synthesize information and think critically.

Clinical Care

In collaboration with our UVM Health partners, we deliver top-tier clinical care for patients. We integrate our research and education missions into network departments, focusing on public health, population health, health services research, and value-based care.
