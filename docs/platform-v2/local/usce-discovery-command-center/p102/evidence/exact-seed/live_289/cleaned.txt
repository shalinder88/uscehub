OHSU News New tool uses single-cell data to predict cancer survival An OHSU team developed a way to map tumor cells and found that some cells drive cancer faster than others. This finding could one day help doctors tailor treatments to patients. Spinal procedures at OHSU just got more precise and efficient OHSU is the first center in Oregon to use a new robotic tool for spine surgeries. The tool helps surgeons place spinal screws more accurately in the spine and pelvis. Pancreatic cancer blood test is 97% accurate, study finds OHSU scientists have created a microchip that analyzes blood samples. The more signs of cancer it finds, the more it lights up. The test could be available to patients in five years. Give to OHSU Your gift to OHSU helps make a healthier world — through research, health care and education.
