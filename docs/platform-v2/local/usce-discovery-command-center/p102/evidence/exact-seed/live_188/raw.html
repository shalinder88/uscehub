Advancing Science and Medicine Through Discovery

00:02:38

Yale School of Medicine advances science and medicine through discovery, in the laboratory and at the bedside and by attracting and nurturing future leaders. We do this by cultivating a dynamic, inclusive community focused on excellence that promotes curiosity and critical inquiry in an environment of scientific rigor.

We are applying advanced methods of research to better understand the complex mysteries of human biology, with an emphasis on creativity, discipline and teamwork, both within Yale School of Medicine and also across the Yale community, and in collaboration with other research institutions.

Through Team science, we bring together different perspectives. The interplay of those disciplines often leads to a reframing of a problem and to creativity.

The translation in translational research is bidirectional, observations in patients drive discoveries in the laboratory, and breakthroughs in research impact human health through clinical trials that ensure safety and efficacy of new therapies, with the goal of promoting health, treating disease, and saving lives.

As we understand the underpinnings of disease, we begin to understand why individual patients develop specific diseases and why these patients respond differently to therapies. This allows us to tailor therapy at the individual and the population level.

To ensure continued innovation and progress, we teach our students how to learn and reason and to value curiosity. This is the philosophy of the Yale System.

We seek collaboration across the university to advance our missions. We also partner with our community of New Haven, the region, and the world to listen and to learn through curiosity and critical inquiry.

Together with Yale New Haven Health, we care for individual patients and also aim to improve the health of all in our region around the country and around the world.

This is the mission of the Yale School of Medicine.

Yale School of Medicine

Yale School of Medicine educates and nurtures creative leaders in medicine and science, promoting curiosity and critical inquiry in an inclusive environment enriched by diversity. We advance discovery and innovation fostered by partnerships across the university, our local community, and the world. We care for patients with compassion, and commit to improving the health of all people.

Research at a Glance

Copy Link

Every day, hundreds of Yale School of Medicine researchers publish in peer-reviewed academic journals around the world. Below is a small selection of highlighted publications.

Cancer Letters | June 2026

HOX Codes and RUNX1T1: Unveiling Lineage Plasticity in Cancer

This study investigates how HOX gene expression patterns identify therapy-resistant cancer subtypes and demonstrates RUNX1T1-HDAC3 inhibition as a potential treatment strategy for aggressive cancers.
Full Summary
  • JAMA Oncol | May 2026

    PSMA-PET Linked to Advanced Prostate Cancer Treatments

    This study investigates how PSMA-PET imaging for prostate cancer leads to earlier use of advanced therapies compared to traditional bone scans, impacting treatment decisions and costs.
    Full Summary
  • Cancer Res | May 2026

    PI3K-Driven Wild-Type RAS Activation Fuels KRAS Inhibitor Resistance

    This study investigates how PI3K enables pancreatic cancer cells to resist KRAS inhibitors by activating wild-type RAS signaling, suggesting combined PI3K-KRAS targeting as a therapeutic strategy.
    Full Summary
  • Cancer Discov | April 2026

    Dietary Fats Shape Pancreatic Cancer Risk via Ferroptosis

    This study investigates how dietary fat composition influences pancreatic cancer, showing that monounsaturated fats promote tumor growth while polyunsaturated fats reduce risk by enhancing ferroptosis sensitivity.
    Full Summary
