Requirements: Valid visa and medical credentials Graduation credentials or proof of medical studies Background check Vaccination records Apply Now Question & Answers How long is the observership? The observership lasts a minimum of 4 weeks. However, to be considered for a strong letter of recommendation from our providers, completing more than 2 months in the program is highly recommended. What Visa do I need? A B1/B2 tourist visa allows you to join our program as an observer only, with no hands-on experience. To participate in hands-on activities, you will need an appropriate work or training visa such as a J-1 or H-1B. Is there a job opportunity after the observership? Job opportunities are not guaranteed. Candidates will be considered for employment in the US if they have permit to work in the United States or remotely if the candidate goes back to their home country. Is there an application fee? No, we do not charge an application fee for our Observership Program in San Diego. However, please note that there is a fee for the observership itself, which is charged per 4-week session. Where is the program located? All activities take place in San Diego, California. Is housing provided? No. Observers arrange their own housing, though we can share local rental contacts upon request. What type of clinical exposure will i get? You will rotate through both outpatient clinic visits and hospital operating-room days to experience the full spectrum between clinical and surgical care. Will my home-country vaccination records be accepted? Yes. As long as you can supply documentation that meets U.S. hospital standards, vaccines and TB testing completed in your home country are valid. Do i need my own malpractice insurance for hands-on experience? No, the physician&#x27;s own malpractice insurance will cover you. For more info, Contact Us Thank you!
