When physicians and researchers can learn from each other and share best practices, patients everywhere benefit. That&rsquo;s the guiding principle behind the International Medicine Institute&rsquo;s Global Observership Program, which offers qualified foreign medical students and physicians one- to three-month rotations at the University to observe the activities of a particular department, division, center or institute. We launched the Global Observership Program in 2008 to build on the successes of the Harrington Program. To date, the program has received over 850 participants from Europe and Asia. We&rsquo;re proud to act as a hub for the international medical community &mdash; and to pass the benefits on to patients from around the world.
