# Resident Rights & Workplace Resources — Policy Document

**Date:** May 2026  
**Phase:** 7 (after community intelligence, well after data moat)  
**Status:** Planning only — no code yet

---

## What this is

A neutral, educational resource lane covering workplace rights, labor law basics, and official resources for medical trainees and attending physicians.

This is **not** a union organizing product, **not** a legal advice service, and **not** a platform for employer-specific campaigns.

---

## What this is NOT — hard boundaries

- Not a union organizing platform
- Not legal advice (no language that could be construed as counsel)
- Not a space for employer-specific campaign coordination
- Not a workplace allegation board
- Not a retaliation resource (no "how to file a complaint against your program" playbook)
- Not CIR's website (link to them, do not replicate their function)
- Not a political positioning statement

---

## Why this exists

Medical trainees routinely encounter situations where they do not know their rights:

- Duty hour violations they don't know how to report
- Moonlighting restrictions they don't understand
- Contract clauses they sign without reading
- Residency training stipends they don't know are negotiable
- Hostile work environments they don't know have reporting channels
- Union activity at their institution they have no context for

USCEHub can serve these users with accurate, source-linked, neutral information — the same "source-linked" principle that applies to every other section.

### 6. Resident Unions — What They Are (neutral explainer)
```
What is a housestaff union?

- Private: NLRB jurisdiction, NLRA rights
- Public: governed by state/federal laws specific to public employees, varies by state
```

What this section explicitly does NOT do:
- Advocate for or against unionization
- Tell users how to organize
- Name specific employers who are "anti-union"
- Recommend joining a specific union
- Reproduce union election materials

Source: public CBAs from CIR and institution disclosures where available.

## Framing guidelines

**Always write:**
- "Under ACGME requirements..." (sourced)
- "NLRB rules..." (sourced)
- "CIR, which represents over 40,000 residents, offers..." (attributed)
- "This varies by institution and state — consult your GME office or an employment attorney"

**Never write:**
- "You should unionize"
- "Your program is violating your rights"
- "You are being exploited"
- "Here is how to file a grievance against Dr. [Name]"
- "This constitutes a hostile work environment" (this is a legal conclusion)
- "Join CIR" (we link to CIR; we do not recruit for them)

## Privacy considerations

If any form-based reporting exists in this lane (e.g., "report a duty hour violation"):
- Anonymous by default
- Do not display individual reports publicly without moderation
- Do not name individual attendings or program directors
- Minimum N before aggregate display
- Do not create a searchable database of program violations
